# SEO Setup Guide - Octopus Energy Referral Page

## ✅ What We've Added

### 1. **SEO Meta Tags in HTML Head**
- `meta description`: Clear description of your offer for search results
- `meta keywords`: Primary search terms (Octopus Energy, referral, £50 credit, etc.)
- `meta author`: Your name/credit
- `meta robots`: Tells Google to index and follow links on your page
- `link canonical`: Prevents duplicate content issues

### 2. **Open Graph Tags (for Social Media)**
When you share this link on Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, etc.:
- Shows an attractive title: "Octopus Energy New Customer Offer - £50 Bill Credit"
- Shows your description automatically
- Better click-through rate from social shares

### 3. **Twitter Card Tags**
Optimizes how your link appears when shared on Twitter with a professional preview

### 4. **XML Sitemap (`sitemap.xml`)**
- Helps Google discover and crawl your page
- Lists your URL with last modified date
- Priority and update frequency information

## 🚀 Next Steps to Get Your Site Ranking

### Step 1: Submit to Google Search Console (IMPORTANT!)
1. Go to: https://search.google.com/search-console
2. Click **"Add property"**
3. Enter your URL: `https://adambennion2-ui.github.io/octopus-energy-new-customer-offer/`
4. Choose **"URL prefix"** option
5. Click **Continue**
6. Follow the verification instructions (add meta tag to your HTML or other methods)
7. Once verified, submit your sitemap:
   - Go to Sitemaps section
   - Enter: `sitemap.xml`
   - Click Submit

### Step 2: Submit to Bing Webmaster Tools
1. Go to: https://www.bing.com/webmasters
2. Add your site
3. Submit your sitemap

### Step 3: Share & Build Backlinks
More shares = Higher ranking. Share on:
- **Reddit**: r/BritishProblems, r/SaveMoney, r/UKPersonalFinance, r/energy
- **Twitter**: Post your referral link with relevant hashtags
- **Facebook Groups**: Energy saving groups, UK consumer groups
- **Forums**: MoneySavingExpert, Which?, energy forums
- **Social Media**: LinkedIn, Instagram (mention the offer)

### Step 4: Monitor Performance
Once indexed (usually 1-2 weeks):
- Check Google Search Console for:
  - How many times your page appears in search results
  - Click-through rate
  - Average position in search results
  - Any crawl errors
